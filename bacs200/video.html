What does it mean to be a Network Administrator? Today we will go over this career path and delve into what it takes to be a Network Administrator. Network administrators are responsible for overseeing their organization’s computer systems and maintaining them. Identifying network and computer system needs, and installing any new hardware or software that is going to be used. As a Network Administrator you will be expected to monitor the network’s security for any potentially malicious or harmful attacks on it. Configure the organizations local and wide are networks. Analyze and resolve end user software and hardware issues. And deploy the wireless infrastructure and supporting systems such as routers, network switches, and other devices. Some of the most important skills you can have as a Network Administrator are a solid foundation in IT and technical skills, good knowledge of both networking hardware and software, problem-solving skills, and good interpersonal communication and teamwork skills so you can effectively communicate with your colleagues and get the job done. The work environment of a Network Administrator can vary greatly depending on the organization but generally they all have a few things in common. You will mainly be in an office environment and a lot of the work is done independently so you have to be self-sufficient. You may also have to work late and odd hours since network maintenance is usually performed during these off-times where less people will be impacted by the outage.
